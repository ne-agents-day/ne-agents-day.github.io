About The Workshop

Autonomous AI agents have emerged as a transformative paradigm in artificial intelligence, enabling systems to perceive, reason, and act within complex environments with minimal human intervention. Powered by advances in LLMs, reinforcement learning, and orchestration frameworks, agents are able to efficiently integrate planning, memory, and tool use in a compute-intensive paradigm to perform multi-step tasks in domains such as software development, healthcare, and education.

Despite recent progress, achieving reliable agent performance in real-world systems remains a comprehensive problem, and coordination across the full stack (ML, systems, and HCI) is underexplored. Progress will require work across:

  • Better model training recipes and agent frameworks;
  • Better system abstractions for both agents and their environments, with a focus on efficiency, scalability, reliability, and observability;
  • Better personalization and human interpretability for seamless human-agent interactions.

The goal of this workshop is to offer a comprehensive overview of AI agents, bring these research communities together to share progress, discuss common problems and evaluation setups, and identify opportunities for collaboration. We aim to bring together attendees from diverse disciplines to foster interdisciplinary collaboration and discuss open research questions.

Call for Papers

We welcome contributions to a broad range of topics on the design, development, systems support, and applications of AI Agents, including but not limited to:

Interfaces and Embodiment

How should agents perceive and act within their environments (e.g., through tool use, exploration, or interactions)? Should we design unified systems or specialized agents for different modalities?

Training Recipes and Data for Agentic Behavior

How can we improve agentic capabilities with more effective training algorithms and better data mixtures? What evaluation-driven training practices best translate into reliable performance on real-world, multi-step tasks?

Safety and Societal Implications

What guardrails are necessary to ensure AI agents operate safely in real-world settings? How can we address the ethical, economic, and societal challenges posed by autonomous agents?

Human-Agent Interaction, Alignment, and Personalization

How can we personalize AI agents to adapt their behavior to dynamic user preferences across individuals, groups, and contexts? How will future interactions between humans and AI agents evolve?

Applications

What are some exciting areas of application for agents? How can we push the frontiers of agentic applications in areas such as computer use, data science, applications for science (e.g., scientific discovery and deep research), education, healthcare, and software development?

Benchmarking and Evaluation

How can we develop robust environments and evaluation metrics that capture the diversity of real-world settings?

Scalable Architectures for Training and Running Agents

What runtime and infrastructure abstractions are needed to train, evaluate, and deploy agents reliably at scale under strict latency, cost, data, and quality requirements?

Context, State, and Orchestration of Agentic Systems

How should agent context and state be represented, managed, and shared over time? What abstractions enable agents to coordinate through shared data, memory, or environments while remaining easily programmable? How do we effectively orchestrate and control multi-agent systems for complex tasks?

Governance, Observability, Debugging, and Reproducibility of Agents

How can we build tracing, provenance, replay, and root-cause analysis tools that make agent behavior inspectable and reproducible—especially under non-determinism and tool/environment drift?
