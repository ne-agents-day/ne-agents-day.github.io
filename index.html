Overview

About the workshop

Autonomous AI agents have emerged as a transformative paradigm in artificial intelligence, enabling systems to perceive, reason, and act within complex environments with minimal human intervention. Powered by advances in LLMs, reinforcement learning, and orchestration frameworks, agents integrate planning, memory, and tool use to perform multi-step tasks in domains such as software development, healthcare, and education.

Despite recent progress, achieving reliable agent performance in real-world systems remains a comprehensive problem. Coordination across the full stack (ML, systems, and HCI) is underexplored. This workshop offers a comprehensive overview of AI agents, brings these communities together to share progress, and identifies opportunities for collaboration.

Call for Papers

North East AI Agents Day 2026

We invite contributions on the design, development, systems support, and applications of AI agents. The goal is to bring together attendees from diverse disciplines to discuss open research questions and evaluation setups.

Better training recipes and agent frameworks

Improving agentic behavior through model training, data, and evaluation-driven optimization.

System abstractions for agents and environments

Efficiency, scalability, reliability, and observability for real-world deployment.

Personalization and interpretability

Human-agent interaction that is aligned, adaptive, and understandable.

Topics of Interest

We welcome submissions on

Interfaces and Embodiment

How should agents perceive and act within environments through tool use, exploration, or interaction? Should we design unified systems or specialized agents for different modalities?

Training Recipes and Data

How can we improve agentic capabilities with better training algorithms and data mixtures? What evaluation-driven training practices translate into reliable performance on real-world tasks?

Safety and Societal Implications

What guardrails ensure agents operate safely in real-world settings? How can we address ethical, economic, and societal challenges posed by autonomous agents?

Human-Agent Interaction

How can we personalize AI agents to adapt to dynamic user preferences across individuals, groups, and contexts? How will future interactions evolve?

Applications

Applications in computer use, data science, scientific discovery, education, healthcare, and software development.

Benchmarking and Evaluation

Robust environments and evaluation metrics that capture the diversity of real-world settings.

Scalable Architectures

Runtime and infrastructure abstractions needed to train, evaluate, and deploy agents reliably at scale under strict latency, cost, data, and quality requirements.

Context, State, and Orchestration

Representation and management of agent context over time, multi-agent coordination, and shared memory or environment abstractions.

Governance and Observability

Tracing, provenance, replay, and root-cause analysis tools that make agent behavior inspectable and reproducible under non-determinism and tool drift.
